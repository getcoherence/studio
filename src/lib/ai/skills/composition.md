# Skill: Video Composition

## When to Apply
When generating a scene plan from a prompt, transcript, or video brief. These rules
govern how scenes are sequenced to create an effective narrative arc.

## Rules

### Narrative Structure
1. **Hook first.** The opening scene (0-3 seconds) must grab attention. Use impact-word,
   ghost-hook, or cinematic-title. Never start with a CTA or logo.
2. **Problem → Solution arc.** After the hook, establish the problem (1-2 scenes), then
   transition to the solution. Use before-after, contrast-pairs, or chat-narrative for
   problems. Use metrics-dashboard, process-ladder, or device-showcase for solutions.
3. **Evidence in the middle.** Place data-driven scenes (metrics, glass-stats, countdown)
   in the middle third. This builds credibility before the CTA.
4. **CTA last.** Always end with a clear call-to-action scene. Never end with a
   text-heavy scene that leaves the viewer with no next step.

### Pacing
5. **Scene duration varies by type.** Text-heavy scenes: 60-90 frames. Data scenes:
   90-150 frames. Visual/cinematic: 80-120 frames. CTA: 90-120 frames.
6. **Alternate energy levels.** Follow a high-energy scene (notification-chaos,
   confetti countdown) with a calmer scene (gradient-mesh-hero, outline-hero).
7. **Total video length.** For marketing content: 15-45 seconds. For explainers:
   30-90 seconds. For demos: 60-180 seconds. Shorter is almost always better.

### Scene Count
8. **Optimal range: 5-10 scenes.** Fewer than 5 feels thin. More than 10 risks
   losing the viewer. For 15-second videos: 4-5 scenes. For 30-second: 6-8.

## Examples

### Good: Product launch (30s)
1. impact-word "Speed" (hook)
2. before-after: old workflow vs new (problem → solution)
3. metrics-dashboard: 3x faster, 50% less cost (evidence)
4. device-showcase: product screenshot (demonstrate)
5. ghost-hook: "Your team deserves better tools" (emotional close)
6. cta: "Try free for 14 days" (action)

### Bad: Everything is hero-text
1. hero-text "Welcome to our product"
2. hero-text "We solve problems"
3. hero-text "Here are features"
4. hero-text "Sign up now"
→ No data, no visuals, no variety. Feels like a PowerPoint.

## Common Mistakes
- Starting with a logo reveal (boring, no hook)
- Putting the CTA in the middle instead of the end
- Using 12+ scenes for a 20-second video (too rushed)
- All scenes having the same background color
- No data-driven scenes (pure text narrative)
